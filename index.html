Ring Road

Massive glaciers visible from the highway, explosive geysers that only hint at the raw energy powering the entire country, and waterfalls so clean you can drink out of their cascading waters, Iceland is a country unlike any I’ve ever visited. It was the fall of 2018 when my siblings and I set out along “Ring Road”, a lonesome highway that circumnavigates the entirety of the island. Over the next two weeks we did our best to experience everything Iceland had to offer and we left wanting to explore even more.

A Storied Parliament

When we first arrived in Iceland, sunset was around 6:00 pm and every day it inched closer and closer to 5:15 pm. So, it was with pink skies and long shadows that we made it to our first stop: Thingvellir National Park, a world heritage site which contains the boundary between the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates. It was in this rift valley that Icelandic parliament met between the 10th to 16th centuries AD. The waterfall here was incredible to behold but we would soon learn Iceland was lousy with breathtaking foss (Icelandic for falls) far grander than this one.

Raw Power

Strokkur is a geyser that erupts with regularity every 6 to 10 minutes. The geothermal forces at work underneath this powerful eruption that can reach up to 40 meters in height are present throughout Iceland. We found many towns along Ring Road boasting gorgeous hot springs with temperatures that were absolutely perfect for lounging in.

Golden Falls

Arriving late was part of the plan when we visited Gullfoss (literally Golden Falls). The twilight sun reflected on these waters made for spectacular colors that seemed to light up the fine mist around the falls with gold dust. Unfortunately, I took a picture of a shadowed area for some reason. Nice.

Straight From The Source

One evening, we arrived after dark to our hostel and were lulled to sleep by the sound of a rush of water we couldn't see. When morning came, though, we saw a waterfall flowing down a massive hill just behind our accommodations. It wasn’t a tourist attraction. It was just one example of the many beautiful waterfalls that dotted the countryside. We decided to explore it. Besides finding a spectacular view from the top, we were thrilled to discover the water to be drinkable and delicious. Me, my brother, and my sister sat on that steep slope for a long time just drinking from the waterfall and snacking on the bilberries that grew wild all around our feet.

Land Of Ice

As we entered Southeast Iceland and its treasure trove of beautiful scenery, a giant glacier was our companion along the highway. In Vatnajokull national park, a fork in the road separates tourists going to visit a waterfall one way and tourists going to see the glacier another way. Although we chose the waterfall, we misread the signs and ended up at the glacier. And we were glad we did.

Entering Another World

While we drove through Ring Road enveloped in a fine fog, listening to the soundtrack of ‘Blade Runner 2049’, we stumbled upon both Diamond Beach and Glacier Lagoon. Literally sitting right next to the road, these popular tourist destinations were just begging us to stop. I had no idea nature could produce such clear ice or such vivid blues.

Fjord Explorer

Fjords were a pleasure to drive through when we were at the bottom. More than a little nerve-wracking while we skirted over and around them, though.

Cruising Amongst The Whales

One of the last things we did was go whale watching. Our cheaper boat tour was unexpectedly cancelled, so they upgraded us to their premium tour on a quicker, more agile craft that let us get right up to the whales.

A Fitting End

In the Westfjords of Iceland, the Dynjandi Waterfall (The Thunderer) is easily the most spectacular site to visit. Incredibly, my siblings and I were among just a handful of people visiting this collection of seven cascades and the only ones that made it to the top waterfall -- a 329-foot behemoth of a fall that resembles a tiered wedding cake. Just like with all other sites in Iceland, we could get as close as we wanted to this natural wonder. And just like those other sites, we drank the delicious water straight from the stream. A fitting end to our trip.
